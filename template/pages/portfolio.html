Portfolio Management

Throughout the financial market industry portfolio management has always been at the core of most financial institution managers’ investment strategies. In the post-2008 environment the capabilities of a manager’s portfolio management solution have been a key differentiator in their ability to generate healthy returns for their investors.

This will intensify in the future whereby a number of factors are coming together in the industry to increase the importance of portfolio management in financial institutions. The events of 2008(also called the subprime mortgage crisis which threatened to destroy the international financial system), where many investors found themselves gated or side-pocketed in hedge funds, have increased the demand for structures that offer more liquidity and which can provide superior portfolio reporting to their investors

Investors have frequently been disappointed with returns from alternative investment funds over the last decade. These funds have sometimes performed worse than expected. This is why Day trading Tanzania will survive and prosper in the future since we have a portfolio management solution at the center of our core competencies (alongside research, asset allocation and risk management).

Our Asset allocation

Since the inception of the funds, DTT has invested in Currencies, Stock indices, Crypto currencies and futures which offer asymmetric payoffs in the event of the occurrence of low probability catastrophic or otherwise unanticipated negative events.

Our risk management approach

In order to monitor risk related to portfolio manager's activities, the board receives a report from the head of trade at each quarterly board meeting or more frequently, as necessary, on developments and risks relating to portfolio positions, activities engagements, financial instruments used in the portfolio and the portfolio composition as a whole.

Risk related to investment activities

With respect to the concentration of the company's portfolio, the board acknowledges that there are numerous risks associated with having a highly concentrated portfolio and that the primary risk management tool used by the portfolio manager is the extensive research performed by the investment team prior to investment., along with the ongoing monitoring of the positions held in the company's portfolio.

Market risk

Adverse changes affecting the global financial markets and economy as a whole may have a material negative impact on the performance of the company’s portfolios. In order to mitigate market-related downside risk the company diversifies its investment in financial assets for a more long-term maturity period and seek to offset any risk that can affect the company in the short term.
