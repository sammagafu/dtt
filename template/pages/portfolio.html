Company Profile

Portfolio Management

Throughout the financial market industry portfolio management has always been at the core of most financial institution managers’ investment strategies. In the post-2008 environment the capabilities of a manager’s portfolio management solution have been a key differentiator in their ability to generate healthy returns for their investors. This will intensify in the future a number of factors are coming together in the industry to increase the importance of portfolio management in financial institutions.

The events of 2008 (also called the subprime mortgage crisis which threatened to destroy the international financial system), where many investors found themselves gated or side-pocketed in hedge funds, have increased the demand for structures that offer more liquidity and which can provide superior portfolio reporting to their investors

Investors have frequently been disappointed with returns from alternative investment funds over the last decade. These funds have sometimes performed worse than expected. This is why Day trading Tanzania will survive and prosper in the future since we have a portfolio management solution at the center of our core competencies (alongside research, asset allocation and risk management).

Our Asset allocation

Since the inception of the funds, we have invested in Currencies, Stock indices, Crypto currencies and futures which offer asymmetric payoffs in the event of the occurrence of low-probability catastrophic or otherwise unanticipated negative events.

Because we cannot predict the economic cycles with precision, we look for asset classes which are capitalized to withstand difficult economic times or even the normal ups and downs of any financial changes to the world economy.

Currencies (forex)

The currency market, or forex (FX), is the largest investment market in the world and continues to grow annually. On April 2010, the forex market reached $4 trillion in daily average turnover, an increase of 20% since 2007. Forex exchanges allow for 24/7 trading in currency pairs, making it the world's largest and most liquid asset market. Day trading Tanzania has incepted 25% of its investment in this asset class which is expected to reach our first milestone in the year 2024.

Stock indices

A stock market index is a section of stocks in a market. It is used by traders and economists to compare returns on different assets, to track the overall economy or as an investment vehicle. Among the most common types of indexes include global indices, regional indices and national indices. Some of the most heavily traded live Indices markets include:

  • The FTSE 100 – Sometimes called the ‘UK 100,’ this represents the UK’s hundred biggest companies by market capital
  • Dow Jones – Often referred to as simply ‘Wall Street’ this comprises 30 of the US’ biggest publicly owned companies
  • The DAX – Referred to as the ‘Germany 30,’ this index is made up of 30 major German companies
  • Nikkei 225 – Japan’s biggest price-weighted index is comprised of 225 of the country’s biggest companies
Crypto currencies

A cryptocurrency is a digital or virtual currency that is secured by cryptography, which makes it nearly impossible to counterfeit or double-spend. Many cryptocurrencies are decentralized networks based on blockchain technology—a distributed ledger enforced by a disparate network of computers.

As many as 17% of the survey's 100 respondents expect more than 10% of their portfolios to be allocated to crypto in 2026, the funds surveyed manage an average of $7.2 billion in assets. North American funds predict their crypto exposure will be 10.6%, while those in the U.K. and Europe forecast 6.8%. If you invest $10,000 in bitcoin today and its value appreciates, say up to $11,000, you stand to make a profit because bitcoin is a digital financial asset. But if its value dips to below $10,000, you will make a loss if you decide to sell. However, you will only profit or see a loss if you sell your asset.

Our investment approach in shortlisting the most potential crypto-assets and add them to our investment portfolios which also fall in line with our strategy for long-term investing.

Futures

Futures contracts originated to help reduce risk for farmers by ensuring a guaranteed price for their crops. The price of a commodity was established on the day of contracting and a small deposit placed by the purchaser to ensure delivery. The actual delivery occurred on a future date, and the outstanding payment was settled. This allowed farmers to establish future prices for commodities such as wheat and helped merchants establish costs in advance of a purchase. Today, managed futures provide direct exposure to international financial and non-financial asset sectors. Trading advisors have the ability to trade in over 100 different markets worldwide. These markets include interest rates, stock indexes, currencies, precious metals, energies and agricultural products. Futures diversifies our portfolio by providing access to products that are hard to find elsewhere. Plus, we can get direct exposure to underlying assets mentioned above. When it comes to trading futures, you’ve got to have transparency and speed. More participation in a market means tighter pricing, lower costs and tremendously efficient trading. This is facilitated by the ongoing partnership between the company and its partner brokers who offer liquidity dark pools which give access to direct market action in real-time.

Our risk management
approach

In order to monitor risk related to portfolio manager’s activities, the board receives a report from the head of trade at each quarterly board meeting or more frequently, as necessary, on developments and risks relating to portfolio positions, activities engagements, financial instruments used in the portfolio and the portfolio composition as a whole.

Risk related to investment activities

With respect to the concentration of the company’s portfolio, the board acknowledges that there are numerous risks associated with having a highly concentrated portfolio and that the primary risk management tool used by the portfolio manager is the extensive research performed by the investment team prior to investment., along with the ongoing monitoring of the positions held in the company’s portfolio.

Market risk

Adverse changes affecting the global financial markets and economy as a whole may have material negative impact on the performance of the company’s portfolios. In order to mitigate market-related downside risk the company diversifies its investment in financial assets that more long-term maturity period and seek to offset any risk that can affect the company in the short term.
